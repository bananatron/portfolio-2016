Philosophy

Tech

As fun as it is to learn cutting edge new frameworks and build complicated systems, I've come to fall in love with simplistic designs and patterns. In the end, users don't care what libraries you're using or how complicated your architecture is - they just know if a product works well. The cognitive load required coming to a complex codebase/feature should be a heavy consideration to growing teams.

I'd rather a hundred developers be able to read the code I've left behind than impress the two that can.

Personal

I'm far from perfect, and the great thing about this industry is that you'll never stop learning. As a result, I think compassion is a key component of team interactivity. I strive to teach and learn without ego, and believe a person can provide massive contributions to a team outside of technical expertise.

A smile goes a long way.

There's always more to learn and more to do.
